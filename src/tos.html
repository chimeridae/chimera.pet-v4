Chimera's Terms of Service

By commissioning me in any form, you inherently agree to these terms of service. Please read them thoroughly beforehand. Thank you!

Curious about anything else? Reach out to me at commissions@chimera.pet!

This page was last updated on: 3-24-2025.

General terms...
  • I reserve the right to decline and refund a commission at any time if I feel uncomfortable or unable to complete your request.
  • Visual reference of some form is required! I won't work on verbal reference alone. It doesn't have to be intricate, though.
  • Sketches will be available to view on my Trello. I don't send updates via DM unless a client requests it, but I may work on your commission on stream sometime.
  • Small edits to some artworks are okay, but I won't do large edits after-the-fact without an extra fee.
  • Finished commissions may be listed as examples. Please let me know ahead of time if you aren't comfortable with it.
  • Make absolutely certain you list a method of delivering your final work, and that you're capable of recieving DMs!
  • Please understand that I am a multiply disabled artist, and energy is a finite resource for me. I'd appreciate your patience.
    • last updated: 2/4/25

Turnaround times...

    Though I typically try to work on commissions right away, please keep in mind that I am a multiply disabled artist (physically and mentally), so my energy can fluctuate.

  • Turnaround times will be different depending on commission types, and are priced accordingly.
  • Without unforeseen circumstances, I try to finish a commission within 2 weeks of starting work, and 2 months at maximum.
  • Any work requiring a rush-order deadline will have an additional fee charged. I may deny your request if I feel that I'm unable to complete it in the requested timeframe.
  • If for some reason I'm unable to finish your commission, I will refund you.
  • Times listed here are purely guesstimates; and the actual turnaround will be based on my energy the time your order is placed. I update my status on my Trello, and occasionally Ko-fi.
    • last updated: 2/4/25

Accepted subjects and payments...

Ko-fi

Ko-fi is my most preferred method of taking commissions!

Pros of Ko-fi:

  • Allows payment through both Paypal and Stripe
  • Gives the option to make smaller donations
  • Has the potential for a membership option, and other fun quirks for donos on my Twitch streams
  • Gives the option for me to sell physical merch in the future

One small con of Ko-fi...

  • Ko-fi immediately takes payment upfront whenever you place your order.
  • This means I may have to refund you after you've paid if I feel unable to complete your request.

If that idea makes you nervous, I would reccomend using Vgen.

Vgen

Vgen is a newer service for me, and is very commissioner-friendly.

Pros of Vgen:

  • Allows payment through both Paypal and Stripe
  • Works by sending requests, and payment only goes through once I accept and begin to work
  • Allows you to request refunds and track my progress

The only real con to Vgen is that it has a non-negotiable fee on my end, and my inexperience with the platform. At the end of the day, it's all up to your personal preference.
